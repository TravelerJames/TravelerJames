I traveled the world to take pictures and change my life.

(TL;DR: I did some cool stuff and want to continue doing more cool stuff.)

I grew up with the environment in mind and to be aware of the biosphere. I took for granted that health and happiness came from the inside and one's relation with the planet. I knew too much in theory.

I had to know it from experience.

When I turned 17, I took a trip to India that ended up lasting 9 months.

I learned Sanskrit and percussion instruments while staying at various houses, hotels and ashrams across the vast country. I traveled by train from Calcutta to the frenetic capitol of New Delhi. I stayed in villages from Agra and made my way up Haridwar and Rishikesh towards the Himalaya mountain range.

I didn't take a camera with me and just lived it.

During my adult life I made a home in the States. I kept busy as an apprentice stonemason, landscaper, wood joiner and photographer.

At 19, I hiked and camped alone on parts of the Appalachian Mountains and lived out of a tent for 6 months. In 2007, I audited a course in Sustainable Innovation and Entrepreneurship at the Darden School of Business.

At 22, I backpacked all over Europe and spent a year in Denmark to learn the national language and more about sustainability. I engaged with local activists and college students in Copenhagen, Roskilde University and at SOAS in London. During the 2009 United Nations Climate Change Conference (COP15), I attended several workshops and participated in building small-scale alternative energy solutions.

In 2010, I received a certificate in creative writing from the University of Oxford in the U.K. and, in the following year, I earned my degree from Piedmont in Virginia.

Afterward, I became an archivist and volunteer.

I organised the personal work of architect and designer William McDonough. In this time, I also entertained several non-profit and volunteer positions in the community. Examples include the special health needs camp for children and the college's organic vegetable garden project.

These experiences helped fortify my resolve to act locally while thinking globally.

I have since contributed prose, poems and photos to various publications.

Notably, my writing has appeared in the Turl Times and the Fall Line. In 2013, my photographs garnered several honourable mentions and nominations from international competitions such as the International Photography Awards, Photography Masters Cup, and Black and White Spider Awards.

I currently reside in Stockholm, Sweden.

I am employed as an image specialist for an electronics wholesale distributor. In my personal time, I learn HTML5 and CSS3 programming and am compiling a book of prose. You can reach me directly by phone or e-mail regarding photography, travel tips or anything else on your mind.
